Most drawer organiser reviews are written by people who have never opened a junk drawer in their life. They show pristine bamboo trays in drawers the size of a small wardrobe, which helps exactly nobody.

I tested 7 drawer organisers in my actual kitchen — the one with the awkward 30cm-deep drawers and the utensil drawer that sticks when it's humid. Here's what worked and what went straight back in the box.

## What Makes a Good Drawer Organiser

Before the list, here's what I was testing for:

- **Adjustable width** — fixed-size organisers are gambling that your drawer matches their dimensions
- **Non-slip base** — if it slides around every time you open the drawer, it's useless
- **Stackable or expandable** — one tray is never enough
- **Doesn't scratch** — cheap plastic scratches wood drawer bottoms

## 1. Bamboo Expandable Utensil Tray

The classic for a reason. Expands from 33cm to 50cm wide, so it fits most standard kitchen drawers. The bamboo is smooth enough that utensils don't catch, and the rubber feet stop it sliding.

**Best for:** Cutlery and utensil drawers.
**Watch out for:** Needs occasional oiling if you live somewhere humid. The expansion mechanism can feel loose after a year.

## 2. Interlocking Mesh Dividers

These clip together in any configuration you want. Buy a pack of 8 and build whatever grid pattern fits your drawer. They're only 5cm tall, so they work in shallow drawers where bamboo trays are too bulky.

**Best for:** Shallow drawers, bathroom drawers, desk drawers.
**Watch out for:** The clips can be fiddly to connect. Not sturdy enough for heavy items.

## 3. Deep Drawer Bins (Stackable)

If you have those deep pan drawers, standard organisers just sit at the bottom looking lost. These bins are 15cm tall and stack two-high, so you can use the full depth of the drawer.

**Best for:** Deep pan drawers, under-sink storage.
**Watch out for:** Measure your drawer depth first — the stacked height is 30cm.

## 4. Spring-Loaded Drawer Dividers

These wedge themselves into the drawer using spring tension. No measuring, no adhesive, no screws. You just squeeze them in and they hold position. Genuinely satisfying to install.

**Best for:** People who rent and can't modify drawers. Quick fixes.
**Watch out for:** They can shift over time in high-traffic drawers. Not great for very wide drawers (over 50cm).

## 5. Silicone Liner + Individual Containers

Instead of one big tray, line the drawer with non-slip silicone mat and place individual containers wherever you want them. It's ugly but effective — you get total control over sizing.

**Best for:** Oddly shaped drawers. The "I gave up trying to find the right organiser" approach.
**Watch out for:** Looks messy if you use mismatched containers. The liner needs washing occasionally.

## 6. Adjustable Pegboard Drawer Insert

A flat board with a grid of holes, plus movable pegs that create walls wherever you want them. This is the most customisable option — you can rearrange the layout in seconds.

**Best for:** Drawers where the contents change regularly. Tool drawers. Craft drawers.
**Watch out for:** Small items can fall under the pegs. The board itself adds 1cm to the drawer height.

## 7. Velvet-Lined Jewellery Tray

Not just for jewellery — these trays work brilliantly for watches, sunglasses, small electronics, or any drawer where you want items to stay put. The velvet lining prevents scratching and sliding.

**Best for:** Bedroom drawers, accessories, small valuables.
**Watch out for:** Hard to clean if something spills. Not suitable for kitchen or bathroom.

## The Verdict

For most kitchens, the **bamboo expandable tray** is still the best starting point — it handles the widest range of drawer sizes and looks decent doing it.

If you have shallow drawers or need flexibility, go with the **interlocking mesh dividers**. If you have deep drawers, the **stackable bins** are the only option that actually uses the space properly.

The spring-loaded dividers are perfect if you rent and need something temporary. The pegboard is best for drawers where contents change regularly.

Skip anything that relies on adhesive to stay in place. It always peels off eventually, leaving a sticky residue that's worse than the original mess.
