Every overlanding build you see on Instagram costs $60-80k and has gear the owner has used exactly once. Dual swingout bumpers, 40-inch light bars, rooftop tents with annexes bigger than my first flat.

That's not overlanding. That's a photo shoot with a truck.

Here's what you actually need to get out there, listed by what matters most. This post contains affiliate links. I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.

## Sleep System — Ground Tent or Rooftop?

Rooftop tents look cool. They're also heavy (50-70kg), expensive ($1,500-4,000), hurt your fuel economy, and make your centre of gravity worse. You also can't drive anywhere without packing them up first.

A good ground tent — like the Gazelle T4 or a simple swag if you're in Aus/NZ — sets up in under 2 minutes and costs a third of the price. I used a ground tent for two years before switching to a rooftop tent, and honestly? The ground tent was more practical.

If you still want a rooftop tent, get a hardshell. Softshells get old fast — the setup and packdown in rain will make you question your life choices.

## Fridge vs Cooler

A 12V fridge (like the Dometic CFX or ARB Elements) is the single best upgrade you can make. Cold drinks, fresh food for days, no ice runs. Budget $600-1,200.

But if you're doing weekend trips only, a quality cooler with block ice works fine. The Yeti tax isn't worth it — an Engel or even a well-packed Coleman will keep ice for 3 days.

## Recovery Gear — Non-Negotiable

Before you buy a single accessory, buy recovery gear:
- **Maxtrax or similar traction boards** — these get you out of sand and mud. The single most-used piece of gear I own.
- **Snatch strap** (not a tow strap — they're different)
- **Rated shackles** — bow shackles, minimum 4.7T rated
- **Tyre deflator and compressor** — airing down is the cheapest traction upgrade. A quality compressor to air back up is essential.

## Storage

Forget custom drawer systems for your first build. Get two or three stackable storage boxes (like Front Runner Wolf Packs) and a cheap cargo net. Organise from there. You won't know what layout works until you've done a few trips.

## Water

Two 20L jerry cans is a solid starting point. One for drinking, one for washing. A gravity filter (like the Sawyer) means you can refill from streams if you're out longer than expected.

## Hot Take: Skip the Awning

Awnings are expensive, heavy, and only useful when you're parked. A cheap tarp and two poles does the same job for $30, packs smaller, and doesn't care if a branch catches it.

## Realistic Budget Tiers

- **Weekend warrior** ($500-1,500): Ground tent, cooler, recovery gear, basic storage
- **Committed** ($2,000-5,000): 12V fridge, rooftop tent or quality swag, drawer system, compressor
- **Full build** ($5,000-15,000): Suspension lift, bull bar, long-range fuel tank, dual battery, water tank

Start at the bottom. Upgrade based on what you actually need after 5-10 trips. Not what looks good in a build thread.
