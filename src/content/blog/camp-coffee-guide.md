Camp coffee advice usually falls into two camps: "Just drink instant" (helpful if you have no standards) or "Bring your entire home setup" (helpful if you enjoy carrying 3kg of coffee gear up a hill).

If you actually care about good coffee but also care about pack weight — and sanity — here's what works. This post contains affiliate links. I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.

## The AeroPress Go Wins. Sorry.

I've tried every method. The AeroPress Go is the best balance of weight (350g with mug), cleanup (rinse and done), and actual coffee quality. It makes something closer to espresso than drip, which means you can drink less and still get the hit.

The built-in mug is the lid. Clever. No extra cup needed if you're going light.

One downside: it only makes one cup at a time. If you're brewing for a group of four, you'll be standing there for ten minutes. For group trips, a percolator makes more sense.

## Percolator — The Group Option

A GSI Glacier 6-cup percolator handles a whole campsite. It's heavier (~400g) and the coffee is objectively worse — more bitter, less nuanced — but nobody's analysing tasting notes at 6am in the cold.

Works directly on a camp stove or fire grate. Nearly indestructible. I've dropped mine off a tailgate twice and it still works fine.

## Pour-Over — For the Committed

A collapsible pour-over dripper (like the Sea to Summit X-Brew) weighs almost nothing and makes genuinely good coffee. The catch: you need to bring filters, and you need patience. Wind is your enemy. Rain is worse.

I use this on solo trips where I have time. It's a ritual, not a quick caffeine fix.

## Just Use Instant (Sometimes)

Steeped coffee bags and Voila or Swift Cup instant are genuinely decent now. Not AeroPress decent, but decent. For ultralight trips or summit pushes, the weight savings are worth the taste trade-off.

## Hot Take: Stop Bringing a Hand Grinder

Pre-grind your beans at home. Yes, freshly ground is better. But a hand grinder adds weight, takes time, and the difference between "ground 2 hours ago" and "ground 2 days ago" is way smaller than coffee people want to admit. Seal it in a ziplock, move on with your life.

## What About the Stove?

If you don't already have a camp stove, a Jetboil Flash boils water in about 90 seconds. It's the fastest path from "just woke up" to "coffee in hand." Heavier than a pocket stove, but the speed is worth it on cold mornings.
